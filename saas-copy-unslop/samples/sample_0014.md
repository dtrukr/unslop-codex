# Problem Section

## The hidden cost of manual drafting

Most small firms are not short on legal skill. They are short on operational leverage.

Manual document workflows create problems that compound over time:

- Attorneys spend too many hours on first drafts that should already be 80% complete.
- Paralegals and staff chase missing client information across email threads, calls, and PDFs.
- Templates drift as different people make different edits in different files.
- Firms rely on individual memory instead of a repeatable drafting process.
- Review time increases because every document starts from a slightly different baseline.
- Turnaround slows, even for matters the firm handles every week.

The result is familiar: longer cycle times, uneven quality, avoidable write-offs, and less room to grow without hiring.
