Unslop profile for React SaaS landing pages.

## Page Skeletons to Avoid

- Do not default to: brand/nav bar, split hero, feature cards, proof sections, final CTA band.
- Do not reuse the sequence: top nav -> eyebrow pill -> huge H1 -> two CTA buttons -> trust stats -> 3-up features -> pricing/testimonials/FAQ -> bottom CTA.
- Do not start every page with left-aligned copy and a right-side dashboard/mockup panel.
- Do not treat "feature section directly under the hero" as the default next move.
- Do not add a wide closing CTA strip or rounded conversion panel near the bottom just because the page needs an ending.
- Do not pad page length with stock pricing, testimonial, comparison, FAQ, or demo-request blocks unless the content genuinely demands them.

## Hero Patterns to Avoid

- Do not use the left-copy / right-product-mockup hero split.
- Do not place a small rounded eyebrow pill directly above the H1.
- Do not use headlines clamped to roughly `10-12ch` just to force 3-5 short lines.
- Do not use oversized H1s with `line-height` around `0.94-0.95` and tracking around `-0.04em` to `-0.06em`.
- Do not drop two CTA buttons under the hero copy as a fixed pair.
- Do not repeat the "primary filled button + secondary outline/light button" cluster.
- Do not place 3 small proof stats, trust badges, or proof snippets directly under the CTA row by reflex.

## Mockups and Media to Avoid

- Do not fake a product screenshot from stacked cards, pills, bars, mini charts, mini tables, or tiny stat tiles.
- Do not build hero visuals from interchangeable SaaS UI atoms with no real product specificity.
- Do not use another hand-built "dashboard card" as the hero media treatment.
- Do not avoid all photography, illustration, or domain-specific imagery and replace everything with abstract UI boxes.
- Do not add a blurred corner orb or pseudo-element glow behind the hero panel to create fake depth.

## Navigation Defaults to Avoid

- Do not use the same three-part nav: logo left, 3-4 links center/right, single CTA far right.
- Do not hide the nav links on mobile and call that a complete responsive strategy by default.
- Do not use another rounded-square logo badge with a centered initial or generic glyph.
- Do not build the logo mark as a `36-40px` rounded square with a gradient fill and one letter inside.

## Section and Component Patterns to Avoid

- Do not default to a 3-up feature card grid on desktop.
- Do not use soft quote cards with one quote, one name, and one role as the testimonial fallback.
- Do not use 2-3 pricing cards with one highlighted plan as the standard monetization section.
- Do not place FAQ accordions in the usual late-page slot between proof and the final CTA.
- Do not keep using minimal low-information footers with just copyright, policies, and one short brand sentence.
- Do not make every surface a rounded card with the same visual weight.
- Do not turn every badge, toggle, label, metric, and chip into a pill.

## Visual Defaults to Avoid

- Do not use the background recipe: two `radial-gradient(...)` corner glows plus one `linear-gradient(180deg, ...)` wash.
- Do not use pale off-white, cream, or eggshell canvases as the default "premium SaaS" background.
- Do not fall back to green or teal as the primary accent for every industry.
- Do not pair green/teal with orange, amber, or gold as the standard contrast accent.
- Do not use dark navy plus cyan/teal glows as the "serious technical" dark-theme variant.
- Do not put diagonal gradients on primary buttons by default.
- Do not rely on faint grid textures as a token attempt at differentiation.

## Card, Radius, and Shadow Slop to Avoid

- Do not give everything large radii in the `18-34px` range.
- Do not round pills to `999px` everywhere.
- Do not keep reusing white or translucent cards with a faint `1px` border and soft shadow.
- Do not keep reusing shadows in the `0 20px 60px` to `0 24px 80px` range.
- Do not apply blur or glass effects to navs, hero shells, or cards as a default finish.
- Do not make every panel feel soft, frosted, elevated, and interchangeable.

## Typography Defaults to Avoid

- Do not default to the usual AI startup font loop: `Inter`, `Manrope`, `Space Grotesk`, `Plus Jakarta Sans`, `Nunito`.
- Do not reach for `Manrope` as the safe modern SaaS answer.
- Do not use serif for one "editorial" word while the rest of the system stays unchanged.
- Do not use monospace only as a tiny technical accent to fake credibility.
- Do not set body copy to the same safe landing-page measure and rhythm over and over: around `18px`, `line-height: 1.68-1.75`, `max-width: 58-60ch`.

## Spacing and Proportion Habits to Avoid

- Do not center the layout around a container of roughly `1140-1180px` with `32-40px` gutters by default.
- Do not keep the hero split at roughly `55/45` or `60/40`.
- Do not make section spacing uniformly airy and symmetrical from top to bottom.
- Do not use the same safe vertical rhythm everywhere just because it looks polished.
- Do not stack CTA rows, proof rows, and card grids with identical spacing intervals throughout the page.

## Code and Naming Defaults to Avoid

- Do not rebuild the page as a single-file prototype with all structure and styling packed together unless that is explicitly required.
- Do not default to React-in-browser via UMD/Babel for this domain.
- Do not recreate the same theme token block categories: `--bg`, `--text`, `--muted`, `--line`, `--accent`, `--shadow`, `--radius`, `--max`.
- Do not reuse the same selector vocabulary: `.nav`, `.brand`, `.brand-mark`, `.hero`, `.eyebrow`, `.btn`, `.panel`, `.hero-card`, `.stats`, `.cta`.
- Do not map arrays into the same interchangeable card grids for features, pricing, testimonials, FAQs, and stats without changing the rendering logic.
- Do not rely on the same responsive collapse rule where the hero simply drops from 2 columns to 1 and the nav links disappear.

## Copy Patterns to Avoid

- Do not open with badge copy like: "Trusted by...", "Built for...", "HIPAA-conscious...", or similar status-label phrasing above the headline.
- Do not use generic proof snippets that exist only to fill a hero row.
- Do not let every section follow the same short-heading, short-paragraph, card-grid rhythm.
- Do not write copy that merely swaps industry nouns into the same landing-page structure.

## Final Check

- If you are about to use any pattern listed above, stop.
- Do not reach for these defaults in a new colorway.
- Instead of repeating any of this template behavior, vary the approach.
- If the work starts to resemble these patterns, change direction.
